This sandbox tool represents the way a society provides for its needs as a matrix of interdependent material and social processes. Rows and columns below represent economic and social sectors. Read rows horizontally to follow how a good is distributed across sectors; read columns vertically to see what a sector requires to function. As presented by Sraffa in his original framework, sectors depend on each other in interconnected, mutually reinforcing ways (e.g. wheat farming needs iron tools, while iron smelting needs wheat to feed workers). These recursive requirements are what make this a provisioning system rather than a collection of isolated activities. The matrix can be seen as a snapshot of an economy in one period, with its parameters indicating whether the economy produces enough materials to replenish itself and reproduce itself into the future.

Select a preset economy above and press Calculate System Parameters to see the structural properties of the system. Then experiment: you can input numbers directly into a cell (inter-sector flow) or in the TOTAL OUTPUT box, add or remove sectors, toggle designations, or right-click cells to annotate integration forms. Click the calculate button again to see the effect of your changes.
