Systemic Reproduction Parameters

Viability, Reproduction and Sectoral Balances (Polanyian Eigenvalue Φ)

Checks if the aggregate output aligns with structural consumption, and if the instituted system achieves physical reproduction (Φ = 1). Unlike Sraffa's λ (which calculates economic surplus on technical goods alone), Φ operates on the entire matrix including social sectors.

Relative Reproduction Values (Left Eigenvector)

Mathematical exchange/provisioning proportions required to replace the inputs consumed and restart the cycle. They act as a relational measure of structural interdependence required for social and economic reproduction.

Gross Sectoral Scale (in Numéraire)

Calculates the total scale of each sector by multiplying its physical output by its relative systemic reproduction value.

Social Appropriation Breakdown

Displays (from 0% to 100%) how aggregate output is divided between basic social provisioning, non-basic surplus consumption, and intermediate goods.

Technical Coefficients Matrix (A)

Values represent the exact physical quantity of the row sector's good required as an input to produce exactly one physical unit of the column sector's good.

Crash Course: Sraffa meets Polanyi

This interactive sandbox merges two traditions in heterodox political economy and economic sociology: Piero Sraffa's structural matrix mathematics, and Karl Polanyi's Substantivist view of the economy as embedded in social institutions. A single formalism captures these different social formations, all of which share an underlying structure: in substantivist terms, all are instituted processes of social reproduction.

1. The Sraffian Foundation: Beyond Marginalism

Sraffa models the economy as a circular, interdependent matrix of production. To produce wheat, one needs iron (for tools); to produce iron, one needs wheat (to feed miners). This matrix allows for the calculation of systemic properties:

  • Viability (λ): Sraffa's original eigenvalue operates on the purely economic/technical matrix. λ < 1 means the system generates a physical surplus; λ = 1 is the subsistence edge case.
  • Relative Exchange Weights: Derived from the left eigenvector of the technical matrix, indicating mathematical proportions required to replace inputs.

2. The Substantivist Turn: Endogenizing Social Reproduction

Sraffa’s core model treats the distribution of the surplus as exogenous. Polanyian economic sociology, however, emphasizes that economic processes are "embedded" in social relations, and that social reproduction is a foundational economic process.

By adding Social Sectors (Households, Communities, The State) directly into the rows and columns, we treat the reproduction of human life and culture as endogenous. This reframes our view of surpluses and viability:

  • The Polanyian Eigenvalue (Φ): Our systemic eigenvalue (Φ) operates on the full provisioning matrix, including social sectors. In a viable society, Φ is constrained to equal 1. The space between Sraffa's economic surplus (λ < 1) and the societal closure (Φ = 1) is precisely where institutional form lives: which social sectors absorb the surplus to achieve systemic balance?
  • Forms of Integration: The institutional overlays in this tool (Market, Redistribution, Reciprocity) map how Polanyi's forms of integration mediate the flows between these structural nodes.
